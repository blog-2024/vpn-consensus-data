    Why Latency Matters More Than Raw Speed

    Many VPN marketing pages boast about “fastest download speed.” For gamers, that’s not the most critical factor. What matters more are:

    • Ping time (ms): Round-trip delay to the game server. Sub-50 ms is ideal for shooters; under 100 ms is playable for most genres.
    • Jitter: Consistency of ping. A stable 45 ms is far better than bouncing between 30 and 150 ms.
    • Packet loss: Even small drops cause rubberbanding or missed inputs. VPN tunneling must handle congestion gracefully.
    • Throughput: Still important for downloads and patches, but latency drives in-game performance.

    Troubleshooting & Setup Tips

    • Use Ethernet: Wi-Fi introduces extra jitter. Hardwire if possible.
    • Pick nearest server: Don’t just choose a country — pick the city closest to the game server.
    • Try protocol swaps: Lightway/NordLynx are usually fastest. Test against WireGuard/OpenVPN if needed.
    • Disable IPv6: Prevents leak issues that can trigger routing instability.
    • Router vs client: Router VPNs cover consoles, but individual apps can be faster. Test both.
